The Philosophy Behind it All

Understand myself, what I believe, and why I believe

Belief is the base to action, and action makes us the human that we are. If you observe your actions you will have noticed you don’t always make the best choices. We all falter, it is part of the process of learning. But through these failures we are given a glimpse of clarity, a glimpse of “what doesnt work.” The trick is to then modify your beliefs and carry on in life, acting along the new accord(this is basically the scientific method). By understanding your beliefs, and why you choose to believe them you are empowered to change them and tweak them at will. You can change how you choose to see the world, and find a model with a broader spectrum of truth. The model through which you choose to see the world, will have major implications in what you are able to accomplish, and will grant you degrees of control over the actions you take in day to day life. Understanding the belief model you use to make decisions and evolving this base, is paramount. (could you be a rocket scientist if you believed the Earth was flat?)


Acknowledgement of Oneness

The connection between us. The existence of a fabric of consciousness that connects all life is not something easily articulated. It is even more difficult to demonstrate. I choose to believe that the connection I feel to the world around me, is as real as anything I can touch. I believe I am a part of something much larger than myself, and so are you. I believe that when you suffer, I suffer, and when I learn, you learn, when we find peace, we find peace. I believe this based on what I have seen, and what I feel. Completely anecdotal, but our lives are anecdotal and subjective experience cannot be dismissed completely. Its not always easy to see this connection, and so I sometimes lose sight of my greater self. It is not always easy to base my actions on this principle, our egos are powerful, and often get the better us us. But as I practice living in Oneness, I choose to act for the good of others, and I see more and more that others choose to act for the good of me.


The web of interdependence spreads far, and quickly.

Why I Walk

I walk too see the world. I walk too meet the people here. I walk to hear their stories and to give a hand where I can. I walk to experience the world in a manner that we are all capable of. I walk to inspire others to follow, to follow their own inspiration and dreams no matter how impossible it may seem. I walk to use my body, my vehicle, my home and my temple, and to strengthen and maintain myself. I walk to push the boundaries of what I know, and to challenge what I believe. I walk to forge and temper a stronger man, a more intelligent man, and a wiser man.


Why I Don't Use Money

It seems, in the eyes of others, the most radical part of my philosophy is the abandon of money. Why would you give up on currency? What is the deal with that? The phrase which floats around is “money is the root of all evil”. We have all heard this, though I don’t believe it. The truth is, currency is a great tool, its invention and introduction made the marketplace a much easier place to navigate. There is nothing intrinsically wrong with money. However there is an inherent problem, inherited from us, and the manner in which we choose to see and use money.

While the dollar is indeed a useful tool, it and our economy dominate the decision making processes on a global level. National policies and local business practices are incentivized to increase profits often at the expense of environmental and human health. For many of us, money also dictates our own personal decision making processes. How many of us live where we want to live, and spend our time doing what we want to be doing? Not many. Our money is more than a currency, our money is the lifeblood of a system which has been built on exploitation and domination of others. The exploitation and domination of us.

Wealth comes in many forms, and the questions is… which wealth is the most valuable to you? Numbers on an ATM screen? Paper dollars? Material assets? Health? Relationships? Happiness? Experiencing the process of living?

I have met many people who seem to prioritize life, based on acquiring wealth in the order I listed above. But I have to wonder, why are we trading the very real experience of living our lives, for the completely intangible numbers on a screen? In my mind the inverse order is much more correct.

Before this adventure starts it seems to me that living without money is an unthinkable, impossible task. I choose to live without money simply as an experiment, and to push the limits of what we believe in as possible. I also choose to live without money because it feels great to be free from the terror of checking your ATM balance.
